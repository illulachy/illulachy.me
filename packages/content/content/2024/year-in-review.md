Looking back on 2023, it was a year of building, learning, and figuring out what I actually want to create. I shipped my first public project, started writing more consistently, and found clarity on the kind of work I care about. This post is my attempt to capture what happened, what I learned, and what I want to carry forward into 2024.

## What I Built

The biggest milestone was shipping the first version of my portfolio site. It had been something I had been putting off for years — waiting until I felt "ready," waiting until I had more impressive things to show, waiting for some undefined threshold. I finally decided to just build it.

The portfolio evolved significantly over the year. It started as a simple Vite + React app with some cards. It became something I am genuinely proud of: an infinite canvas timeline using tldraw that lets visitors pan and zoom through my chronological journey. Building it taught me more about performance, state management, and creative technical constraints than any tutorial ever could.

I also spent a lot of time with tldraw, learning how to build custom shapes and integrate them into a larger application. The tldraw API is genuinely well-designed — it handles so much complexity around pan/zoom, rendering, and event handling that would have taken months to build from scratch.

## What I Learned

**Shipping beats perfecting.** I have a natural tendency toward over-engineering and perfectionism. In 2023 I practiced the discipline of shipping things that felt 80% ready. The feedback and momentum from shipping even imperfect work is worth more than the marginal improvement from another month of refinement.

**Writing clarifies thinking.** I started keeping a more consistent writing practice this year — notes, drafts, this blog. I was surprised by how much the act of writing helped me understand my own thinking. When I cannot explain something in writing, it is usually because I do not actually understand it yet.

**Depth over breadth.** Early in my career I tried to learn as many technologies as possible. I have shifted toward going deep on a smaller set of things. Understanding TypeScript well enough to write type-safe generic utilities, understanding React deeply enough to reason about rendering behavior — these pay more dividends than spreading attention across 20 different tools.

**Community matters.** I started engaging more with the developer community in 2023 — following interesting people on Twitter, reading blogs carefully, occasionally sharing work. The quality of my thinking improved noticeably. Good communities expose you to ideas you would not encounter in isolation.

## What Did Not Go Well

I started and abandoned several projects. A habit tracker, a small CLI tool, a browser extension. I have a pattern of getting excited about something, building the interesting part, and then losing interest when it comes to the polish and distribution work. This is something I want to improve.

I also struggled with consistency in my learning practice. I had periods of intensive focus followed by weeks where I barely wrote code outside of work. The peaks were great but the valleys were frustrating. Building more sustainable habits remains a goal.

## Looking Forward

For 2024, I want to:

1. Continue building the portfolio site — the blog integration, improving the content pipeline, and making it feel more alive with regular updates
2. Write more consistently — aim for at least two posts per month
3. Go deeper on the things I care about: TypeScript, React architecture, and web graphics
4. Ship at least one project to real users who are not me

The portfolio site is my main creative outlet right now. It is where I get to make genuinely interesting technical decisions and build something that feels distinctly mine. I am excited to see where it goes.

## Gratitude

Thank you for reading. Building in public feels vulnerable, but it has been one of the most valuable practices I have developed. The small acts of sharing work, writing about what I learn, and showing up consistently — they add up to something real over time.

Here is to 2024.
